## Dates

Late first century CE

## Garden Description

The facade of this modest house of a *villa rustica* near the [Meuse river](https://pleiades.stoa.org/places/109180) measured 24 m. in length and had two protruding wings joined by a portico (Plan 1). The house was built in the late 1st century CE. There is evidence for an arbor or pergola next to the house in the form of two rows of postholes arranged diagonally and converging in front of the entrance on the west (circles on plan). Between the rows of posts, each measuring ca. 15 m. in length, and around which some kind of vines or other climbing plants may have been trained to grow, was a small pool.
