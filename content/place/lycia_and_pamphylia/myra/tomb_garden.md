## Garden Description

A broken tombstone from Myra <!--[(Petersen-Luschan, Reisen II 36,56)](https://inscriptions.packhum.org/text/283424?hs=146-150%2C178-183)-->records part of a testator’s instructions to certain persons (gardeners?) charged with “not living elsewhere but staying in the heroon and themselves taking care of it completely” (*ἀλλαχῆ οἰκῆσαι, μένειν δὲ ἐν τῶ ἡρώω καὶ αὐτοὺς ποιεῖσθαι τὴν ἐπιμέλειαν αὐτοῦ πᾶσαν*) in return for which “they are to have use of the walled garden and the buildings in it, two underground rooms, and the two corresponding rooms on the ground floor” (*οἳ καὶ ἐχέτωσαν τὴν χρῆσιν τοῦ περιτετειχισμένου κηπίου καὶ τῶν ἐν αὐτῷ οἰκημάτων, οἴκων καταγείων δύο· καὶ τῶν κατ’ αὐτῶν ἀναγείων δύο*). The two “houses” (*oikoi*) were probably humble structures, combining storage rooms for garden equipment and residential space for the gardeners.
