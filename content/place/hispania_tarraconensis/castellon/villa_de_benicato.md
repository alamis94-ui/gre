## Excavation Dates 

1883; 1955

## Location Description

Nules is about 47 km north-northeast of Valencia. The villa lies 2.4 straight-line km south-southeast of the center of town and 2.95 km back from the present coastline. Surrounded by orange groves, it was not yet, in 2007, officially visitable. It was, however, in the care of the Servicio de Investigaciones Arqueológicas y Prehistóricas of the Province of Castellón, which planned, after proper preparation, to open it to the public. Rooms are clearly outlined by walls about 50 centimeters high.

## Garden Description

The first finds go back to 1883.  Part of the site was acquired by the provincial government in 1954 and archaeological work was begun the next year, but the reports remain unpublished. The villa from the middle of the 2nd century evolved around a peristyle garden with a large circular pool, 7.6 meters in inside diameter, in the middle.  A paved corridor led to the pool from the north gallery of the peristyle.  There were baths and two rooms with mosaic floors.  Most rooms, however, had floors of hammered earth.
